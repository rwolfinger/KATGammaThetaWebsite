The Gamma Theta chapter of Kappa Alpha Theta was installed at Carnegie Mellon University in 1944, made up of the sisters of Beta Tau Lambda which had been on campus for 23 years. In 2004, the Gamma Theta chapter celebrated its 60th anniversary on campus. Thetas are a huge presence in the Carnegie Mellon community, and we are committed to continuing this tradition for years to come!

Membership
Theta is currently the largest sorority at Carnegie Mellon. We currently have 94 members here with one studying abroad. We also have an active group of alumni and advisors. Theta has become a prominent organization on Carnegie Mellon's campus by hosting and participating in large charity events and Greek community events.

Home Sweet Home
In 2005, our chapter was given the opportunity to move into a fabulous newly remodeled facility on the Fraternity Quadrangle. Our home is located at 1077 Morewood Ave. and is leased by CMU Housing and Dining. The house features 6 single rooms and 11 double rooms, allowing housing for 28 sisters. The first floor is equipped with a spacious kitchen, complete with all necessary cooking and baking supplies.
The casual living room has a large flat-screen TV and enormous cushy couches - perfect for sisters to settle in for Glee Tuesdays! A second formal living room provides space for meetings and schoolwork. Since these living areas are extremely conducive to exciting activities and chatter, there is a study room on the first floor for those who prefer a quiet place to work. In the basement are an exercise room and a huge chapter meeting room.
